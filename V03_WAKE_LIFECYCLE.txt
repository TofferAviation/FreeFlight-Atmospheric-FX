FFATMOSPHERICS v0.3 - WAKE LIFECYCLE
====================================

Stage 1 - Jet/core phase
------------------------
Particle: ffatmo_contrail_core
Typical visible age: 0-12 seconds
Behavior: dense, narrow engine plumes with fast formation and fade.

Stage 2 - Primary vortex wake
--------------------------------
Particle: ffatmo_primary_wake
Typical visible age: about 7-130 seconds
Behavior: delayed fade-in, opposite left/right corkscrew emission, gradual
descent, expansion and vortex instability. Lifetime shortens in drier air.

Stage 3 - Secondary ice curtain
--------------------------------
Particle: ffatmo_secondary_curtain
Typical visible age: about 12-300 seconds in persistent conditions
Behavior: ice detrained above the descending primary wake remains near the
original flight level, broadens and becomes increasingly turbulent.

Stage 4 - Contrail-cirrus transition
------------------------------------
Particle: ffatmo_contrail_cirrus
Typical visible age: delayed until roughly 25-55 seconds, then up to the
quality/persistence cap
Behavior: broad, low-opacity, irregular ice-cloud population. It is suppressed
when relative humidity over ice is insufficient for persistence.

Important first-test observations
---------------------------------
- Engine and wing emitter alignment.
- Whether the paired primary wakes visibly rotate in opposite directions.
- Whether descent is subtle and believable rather than a rapid fall.
- Whether the secondary curtain is visible but less dense than the primary wake.
- Whether the cirrus layer begins late enough and breaks up naturally.
- Frame rate after flying straight for at least three minutes on High quality.

This is a native-particle approximation of the visible lifecycle. X-Plane does
not expose interacting ice particles or a fluid solver, so it is not CFD and it
does not create a weather-system cloud.
